Tom Collins Recipe

Tom Collins cocktails taste like grown-up lemonade.

They’re irresistibly citrusy, fizzy and refreshing, with herbal notes from the gin. While lemonade can be cloying, Tom Collins need not be too sweet—I like mine with just a splash of simple syrup to round out the flavors

This is the Elderflower Tom Collins: Try substituting St. Germain (an elderflower liqueur) for some or all of the simple syrup, to taste.

Ingredients

  • Ice cubes
  • 60 ml Gin
  • 30 ml Lemon Juice
  • 15 ml St. Germain
  • 1 bottle of Soda water
  • lemon, orange round, cucumber or mint leaves for garnish
  • Cocktail cherry for garnish
  • Cocktail shaker

Steps

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice. Pour in the gin, lemon juice and St.Germain liqueur. Shake for 20 seconds until frost forms around the shakrer
  2. Fill up a tall cocktail glass with ice. Strain the contents inside the shaker into the glass and top up with soda water
  3. Stir the glass with a long spoon
  4. add your garnish to add some presentation to the drink
  5. Enjoy!
